AI Music Generation
Comparing classical piano music generated by RNN vs LM models

Terminal Chess

Pale Metadata Tool
Quote-finding and chapter metadata for J.C. McCrae's Pale
  • An efficient tool allowing readers to search for quotes/evidence in the murder mystery web serial Pale, by J.C. McCrae

My Journey

Where I've worked, what I've done, and how I got here.

  • Fall 2018

    Entering Cornell

    I began as a student in Cornell's College of Engineering in Fall 2018. I soon affiliated with the Computer Science major, and have taken courses in data structure, algorithms, functional programming, natural language processing, AI, machine learning, computer vision, system security, formal verification, and more!

  • Summer 2019

    Contractor With HCA/Parallon

    At HCA, I worked on the Strategic Data Services team, using SQL to write scripts for data management and analysis. I had three major projects while working for HCA.

    First, I worked independently with a key data table that tracked resource usage. I identified inconsistencies and redundancies in the data and wrote a SQL tool that stadardized the data while also reducing its size by 90%, allowing underutilized resources to be identified.

    Second, I used SQL to automate a previously manual analysis of a 30-million-record table to evaluation collections performance across facilities.

    Finally, I designed and developed a chatbot AI in Python that could decode complex user messages to generate and run SQL queries, enabling efficient access to insurance denials data for teams that were not proficient in SQL.

  • Fall 2019

    Began as TA for CS 2800: Discrete Structures

    My Sophomore year was the start of my TA journey! I first discovered my passion for teaching as a TA for Discrete Structures, a core requirement for the CS major. In my first year as a TA, I taught a discussion section, held office hours, and contributed to grading around 300 homework submissions every week.

  • Summer 2020

    Google STEP Internship

    At Google, I worked with a pod of two other interns to design, create, and present a full stack metadata dashboard application intended to aid consumers in visualizing the long-term impact of accepting Google Cloud Platform IAM Binding recommendations.

    My main role in this project was designing and implementing the database layer of the application, using Java, SQL, and Google Bigquery. I also designed a data pipeline for automated dashboard data updates.

  • Fall 2020

    Began as TA for CS 4820: Algorithms

    In my junior year, I applied to TA Algorithms, another core CS course. As a TA for this class, I again held office hours and contributed to grading around 400 submissions per week. Since school was hybrid for this year, I also stepped up to be the 'chat TA', answering students' questions during lecture in the Zoom chat! After 4 semesters of holding office hours and finding the best way to answer students' questions, I think I developed my communication skills quite a bit.

  • Summer 2021

    Google SWE Internship

    At my second Google internship, I created a metadata service that simplified access to information about a YouTube Ads data repository. The service took the form of a server and client command-line tool written in C++. I fully designed the project, and learned new technologies to work within the existing company infrastructure to be able to set up regular updates for the deployed tool and automated ongoing testing. I also created a monitoring dashboard to report latency, QPS, and error rates, as well as fire alerts on abnormal behavior.

  • Spring 2022

    Head TA for CS 2800: Discrete Structures

    In my last semester of college, I switched back to working as a TA for Discrete Structures, this time as a Head TA. In this role, I not only continued my regular duties of running a discussion section, holding office hours, and grading, but I also managed a grading group of undergraduate TAs, wrote grading rubrics, responded to regrades requests, and managed other logistics within course staff. Working in this role helped me develop my organizational and leadership abilities!
